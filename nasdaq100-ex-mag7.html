The Great Convergence: Mag 7 vs. The Rest

The dominant narrative of 2026 is "The Great Convergence" — the earnings growth gap between the Magnificent 7 and the rest of the market is collapsing. The Mag 7's ~18% projected EPS growth is their slowest since 2022, while the S&P 493's ~13% growth is the strongest since 2021. By late 2025, non-Mag 7 stocks already represented 59% of the S&P 500's annual return, reversing three years of Mag 7 dominance. All 16 Zacks sectors are expected to show positive earnings growth in 2026 — the first time since 2018.

NDX Breadth Is Expanding (Unevenly)

  • Mag 7 = ~40% of NDX weight, but only 22% of NDX names beat the benchmark in 2024
  • NDX stocks above 20-day MA rose from 33% → 53% (Dec 31 → Jan 26)
  • AI hardware/infra names are leading the broadening
  • SaaS sector is narrowing breadth — "SaaSpocalypse" crushed traditional software
  • Cboe launched the "Magnificent 10" Index adding AVGO, AMD, TSMC to Mag 7
  • Equal-weight NDX (QQEW) still lagging cap-weight — but gap expected to narrow

Bottom Line

The Nasdaq 100 ex-Magnificent 7 is experiencing a dramatic sector rotation. AI infrastructure companies (Broadcom, Micron, Applied Materials) are thriving as the direct recipients of $660B+ in hyperscaler spending. Meanwhile, traditional SaaS companies face an existential "SaaSpocalypse" as AI agents threaten the per-seat model. The market is bifurcating into AI builders (semiconductors, platforms) and AI victims (legacy software). The biggest opportunities lie in companies that are either critical to AI infrastructure or successfully pivoting to AI-native business models. The biggest risks are extreme valuations (Palantir at 200x+ PE) and the possibility that hyperscaler capex peaks in 2026.
